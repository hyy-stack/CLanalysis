# Anrok Discovery Context

This document is the authoritative reference for Anrok's discovery methodology. It contains the complete Net-New and Rip-and-Replace discovery playbooks, all six differentiator trap-setting sequences, and the discovery call scorecard. Use this to evaluate rep execution and coach toward Anrok-specific language and questions.

---

## SECTION A: NET-NEW DISCOVERY PLAYBOOK
*Apply when buyer has NO existing tax engine (Greenfield scenario)*

### Buying Audience
CFO, CAO, VP of Finance, Controller, Head of Tax

### Value Drivers
1. **Mitigate Financial Compliance Risk** — Penalty fear, audit exposure, board/investor concerns, personal CFO liability
2. **Build Financial Infrastructure for Scale** — Fundraising readiness, M&A, IPO prep, scaling into new jurisdictions
3. **Grow Global Revenue** — International expansion, removing compliance as a blocker to new markets

### Before Scenarios (current state pain to surface)
- Lack of visibility into level of risk and exposure
- No understanding of actions to take once risk is identified
- Don't know how to expand business safely and compliantly
- Lack of resources, time, and budget for implementation and monthly management
- Fear of negative revenue impact by adding tax to invoices
- Received a tax notice or living in fear of surprise tax notices

### Negative Consequences (quantify in the buyer's own words)
- Paying taxes on behalf of customers out of pocket
- Incurring penalties and interest from late or inaccurate filings
- Risk of unexpected cash outflows from tax notices and audits
- Reduction in valuation due to tax liability (investor/M&A impact)
- High inefficiency and risk associated with manual processes
- Non-compliance slowing funding, growth, and expansion
- Increased personal and reputational risk to the CFO/Controller

### After Scenarios (get buyer to articulate these)
- Clear visibility into tax risks and liabilities
- Trust that they have support when needed
- Clean data to support easy, on-time close
- Tech stack that is at the forefront of AI
- Compliance they don't have to think about or hire for

### Positive Business Outcomes
- Become compliant before a notice or audit arrives
- Money saved on penalties = more investment and better margins
- Ready for fundraising, acquisition, IPO, or audit at any time
- Prepared to scale into new jurisdictions without friction
- Lean, innovative finance team with hours recaptured

### Required Capabilities (establish before pitching)
1. Compatible technology with their financial stack
2. Partner that can support in any jurisdiction
3. One vendor to cover all tax jobs and close the books
4. Ability to evolve and support as complexity grows
5. Accurate and easy to use
6. Experience working with the most innovative organizations
7. Accessible, responsive support and expertise
8. Reliable, useful data that is audit-ready

### Success Metrics
- Exposure and P&I accruals mitigated
- % compliance achieved
- Time to integration complete (billing system connected)
- Hours spent manually adjusting
- Accuracy and timeliness of filings
- Time to kick off a new registration
- Support response time

### Verbatim Discovery Questions

**Tier 1 — Current State (Before Scenarios + Negative Consequences)**
- "What's taken place that's caused you to reach out to us?"
- "How important is it to achieve compliance in the near future?"
- "Describe your level of visibility into where you're exposed. What's the liability you've incurred? What level of financial risk are you comfortable with? How do you think the board or executive team would respond?"
- "What is your existing process to address exposures? Tell me about your level of urgency to act on the exposure."
- "Describe how you close your books. How satisfied are you with the speed and efficiency of that process today?"
- "Describe the tech stack in your finance org. Describe for me the flow of data between your systems when a transaction happens. What plans do you have for upcoming changes?"
- "What is your growth strategy for the next 12–24 months? What are your questions or concerns when thinking about this growth strategy?"
- "Tell me about your fundraising plans in the next 1–2 years."

**Tier 2 — After Scenarios (desired future state)**
- "Describe your ideal scenario with tax compliance. What does great look like?"
- "How might resolving these issues impact the way the CEO perceives the finance team?"
- "Describe a more efficient book-closing process."
- "If you were able to resolve your tax liabilities, how would you invest in the business?"
- "Describe for me your confidence in your finance team's ability to scale in the next few years."
- "What are some of the outcomes of more visibility into your risk?"

**Tier 3 — Required Capabilities**
- "To achieve the outcomes we discussed, what are you looking for in a solution?"
- "What would you need to fix the gaps in your current financial stack?"
- "How would you like to see your global strategy supported?"
- "What level of support are you looking for in a partner?"
- "How important is it for you to have a vendor that has a proven track record of working with the most innovative companies?"
- "How important is it to avoid needing multiple vendors or tools in a highly resourced process?"
- "How would you measure the success of a project? What metrics?"

### Proof Points for Net-New
- **Anthropic and Cursor** — Adding billions in revenue annually. Needed to quickly become sales-tax and VAT compliant across the globe without a built-out finance team. Faced complex, varying tax rules across jurisdictions. *Solution:* Anrok, including no-code integration with Stripe for billing and payments. *Results:* Global rollout of sales tax and VAT enabling compliance in under an hour per month while supporting rapid growth.
- **Vanta** — "With Anrok, we have better control over our global tax compliance. Anrok saves us countless hours each month, and helps us achieve critical accuracy." — David Eckstein, Chief Financial Officer

## SECTION B: RIP-AND-REPLACE DISCOVERY PLAYBOOK
*Apply when buyer has an EXISTING provider — Avalara, Vertex, TaxJar, or homegrown solution*

### Buying Audience
CFO, CAO, VP of Finance, Controller, Head of Tax

### Value Drivers (same three, different manifestation)
1. **Mitigate Financial Compliance Risk** — Existing vendor is creating risk, not eliminating it; errors, gaps, lack of monitoring
2. **Build Financial Infrastructure for Scale** — Current vendor can't scale; patchwork of tools creating complexity
3. **Grow Global Revenue** — Current vendor lacks global coverage or makes international compliance too manual

### Before Scenarios (competitor-specific pain)
- Multiple vendors and unpredictable costs
- Slow and inaccessible support (wait days during a close)
- Inaccurate tax calculation and return errors requiring manual correction
- Lack of reporting and visibility into global compliance
- Stressful, manual work required outside of the platform (spreadsheets, workarounds)

### Negative Consequences (quantify these)
- Surprise cash outflows from liability gaps the vendor missed
- Unexpected software or vendor fees not in the original contract
- Audits take significant time and resources to defend and complete
- Monthly closes delayed because of tax data cleanup
- Operational impact due to slow vendor response on urgent issues
- Resources and time wasted on validation and error correction

### After Scenarios (get buyer to articulate these)
- One responsive, accessible team to contact
- Accurate software that enables quick and easy book closing
- Data and tools that the team likes and uses
- Clear, predictable pricing — no surprises
- Easy integration with current and future tech stack

### Positive Business Outcomes
- Transform the productivity of the finance organization
- Reallocation of time spent on monthly close to strategy
- Reduce total cost of ownership (vendor consolidation)
- Better prepared to scale and future-proof the business
- More accurate budget forecasting and planning

### Required Capabilities (establish before pitching)
1. Easy to migrate — minimal engineering lift, low disruption

## SECTION C: DIFFERENTIATOR TRAP-SETTING SEQUENCES
*Use these to shape decision criteria before pitching. Always: Set Topic → Open Trap → Close Trap*

### 1. Built and Supported by Tax Experts [Holistic]
**Description:** Built by Big4 tax experts from the ground up. Direct access to Big4 tax experts whenever needed. Industry-leading integration partners and accounting firms trust Anrok with their clients.

**Customer Value:**
- Cost: Access Big4-level expertise without additional costs; fast support on complex edge cases to avoid growing liabilities
- Risk: Proactive monitoring of regulatory changes; reduce out-of-pocket liabilities from incorrect tax configurations

**Trap-Setting:**
- **Set Topic:** "Walk me through the support you receive from your current solution when tax challenges or questions come up."
- **Open Trap:** "How often do you feel the support you receive actually answers your questions? Tell me how often your questions or concerns are addressed quickly. To what level do you need to leverage accounting partners or external parties to get answers to compliance questions?"
- **Close Trap:** "What level of support would you like to see from your provider? What would give you confidence the product and support you receive can meet the needs of your business?"

**Additional Trap:**
- **Set Topic:** "Explain what tax scenarios have been difficult to configure and support with your existing tax system."
- **Open Trap:** "What tax edge cases are you unable to easily support? What level of external tax expertise is required?"
- **Close Trap:** "What would you want in a solution to better support the complexity of your environment?"

### 2. AI-Automated and Audit-Ready [Comparative]
**Description:** Compliance you can prove with audit-ready reports and transparency. AI-powered efficiency with Big4-verified accuracy. $50B+ in revenue under proactive compliance.

**Customer Value:**

## SECTION D: 10-POINT DISCOVERY CALL SCORECARD
*Internal coaching reference — evaluate rep execution against these dimensions. Do not output scores to the rep.*

Each dimension scores 0, 1, or 2:
- **2** = Fully achieved — specific, buyer-articulated, confirmed
- **1** = Partially explored — implied, vague, or rep-stated rather than buyer-confirmed
- **0** = Absent — not explored or skipped entirely

| # | Dimension | What "2" Looks Like |
|---|-----------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Before Scenarios** | Specific buyer-voiced current state (concrete who/where/when/how often) — not generic complaints |
| 2 | **Negative Consequences** | Quantified business impact: $, hours, % risk — not just "hurting margins" |
| 3 | **After Scenarios** | Buyer explicitly describes specific future-state (behavior/process changes with targets), not just "be more efficient" |
| 4 | **Positive Business Outcomes** | Expressed in revenue/cost/risk terms by the buyer ("Improve time to detect by 45%", "Cost savings 30% sustained") |
| 5 | **Required Capabilities** | Two or more explicit "must-statements" from the buyer, tied to their pain/outcomes |
| 6 | **Success Metrics** | KPI + baseline + target + timeframe stated by the buyer ("cut incident response 8→5 hours this quarter") |
| 7 | **Mantra Recap** | Delivered in buyer's language, with explicit confirmation before solution pitch — not AE-centric summary |
| 8 | **Question Quality** | Open-ended (Tell/Explain/Describe/Walk me through), two-sided, cause-and-effect probes throughout — no leading or multi-barrel questions |
| 9 | **Economic Buyer & Champion** | Economic Buyer clearly identified AND Champion identified with evidence of advocacy and willingness to sell internally |
| 10 | **Next Steps** | Specific: owner + date/timeframe + purpose tied to business outcomes — all three present |

**Score interpretation:** 16–20 = Strong | 10–15 = Developing | 5–9 = Weak | 0–4 = Not Performed

**Good question patterns:** "Walk me through…", "Describe…", "Tell me about…", "Explain to me…", "Help me understand…"
**Poor question patterns:** Leading questions, rapid yes/no checks, multi-barrel questions (asking several things at once), feature pitches disguised as questions
